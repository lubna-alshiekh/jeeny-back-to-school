2. Historical analysis translated into campaign structure

The campaign design follows the strongest patterns seen in previous Jeeny driver campaigns.

Retained drivers are the volume anchor

Retained drivers generated the majority of converted rides and showed the strongest resilience at higher milestones. They keep the 6-rides-per-day target and receive access to the full premium journey.

New drivers need an early win

Newly boarded drivers respond well but drop when the first meaningful milestone is too far away. Their 5-rides-per-day target earns an immediate sticker pack and daily-draw entry.

Reactivated drivers need low-friction motivation

Reactivated drivers showed the sharpest early drop-off. Their 3-rides-per-day target creates a quick win and gives them the same chance to collect cards and enter daily draws.

How the estimates work: total card drops = expected packs × cards per pack. Expected card drops = total card drops × card probability. Approximate drivers receiving a card at least once uses the probability of getting that card across all average drops earned by one achiever.

How the probabilities were selected

Common cards carry the highest probability so drivers see fast progress. Uncommon cards slow completion slightly, while Rare and Legendary cards preserve anticipation and keep the full album valuable. The total equals 100% across all nine cards.

The model is designed so a driver earning several packs has a strong chance of collecting most cards, but still needs consistency—and occasionally missing-card protection—to complete the album.

Recommended qualification

Daily: Reach the cohort ride target to unlock one three-card pack and one daily-draw entry.

Weekly: Collect at least seven unique cards to enter the weekly cash draw.

Final: Complete all nine unique cards to enter the premium draw.

Protection: After pack seven, increase the chance of missing cards to reduce excessive duplicates.
